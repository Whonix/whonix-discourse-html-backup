I have no idea. Don’t except a solution.

Note: grub-live isn’t a totally new invention by Whonix. You’re in no way asking the only people who might know this. Packaging aside, grub-live is effectively just under 30 lines of code. Package grub-live is merely an usability enhancement of existing functionality. I am not saying it was easy to invent but there core parts were even harder and developed elsewhere. Therefore you have the option of going back to the origin, Debian Live Project.

By comparsion, an arbitrarily chosen Debian live-config script is ~ 330 lines of code and in folder /lib/live/boot alone there are 24 scripts.
