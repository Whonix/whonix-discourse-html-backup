OCSP Must-Staple requires OCSP Stapling for the certificate. If a browser comes into contact with a certificate without OCSP Stapling, it will be rejected. Not only does Must-Staple mitigate the threat of downgrade attacks, it also reduces unnecessary traffic to the CAâ€™s OCSP responders, improving responsiveness and overall OCSP performance.

Est. reading time: 1 minute

OCSP Must-Staple makes use of the recently specified TLS Feature Extension. When a CA adds this extension to a certificate, it requires your browser to ensure a stapled OCSP response is present in the TLS handshake. If an OCSP response is not present, the connection will fail and Firefox will display a non-overridable error page.
