That is a 2-year old fork of the Tails‚Äô project. The official Tails repository is here: https://git-tails.immerda.ch/onioncircuits/

But‚Ä¶ in general, you should look first in Debian repositories because:

  1. packages will be signed
  2. packages will be updated with the rest of your system

onioncircuits is in the jessie-backports repo. Instructions. Is also included in debian stretch and Whonix-14.

Note on Bridges:

Bridges can be useful for circumventing censorship (ie ISP filtering). They don‚Äôt increase your anonymity and they don‚Äôt hide Tor usage from somewhat sophisticated adversaries. (https://blog.torproject.org/research-problems-ten-ways-discover-tor-bridges)

You shouldn‚Äôt use a bridge unless you have difficulty accessing the Tor network:

  1. Bridges tend to be slower and less reliable than entry guards.
  2. Bridges have limited bandwidth that should be reserved for people who need them.
